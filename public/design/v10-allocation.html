Why

The macro setup driving this allocation.

Three macro composites are aligned for the first time since 2022 — Risk & Liquidity calm, Growth firming, Inflation & Rates neutral. That's the setup the back-test calls "lean in," and the allocation reflects it.

Risk & Liquidity sits at −6.7 (calm). Credit spreads have tightened 90 basis points since January. Short-end funding eased as the Fed signaled patience. The composite has improved 11 points over the quarter — that's the green light to take risk.

Growth is firming at +3.2. ISM Manufacturing finally crossed 50 for the first time since 2022. Jobless claims flat at trend. Capex revisions across mega-cap tech are positive. The economy isn't booming, but the broad-based growth signal is what the model needs to add equity beta.

Inflation & Rates is neutral at −1.1. Not stressed, but not cooperative either — 10-year yields are range-bound near 4.5%. That's why the model holds 14% in defensive ballast (cash, short-duration treasuries, gold) and stays underweight rate-sensitive industries like Real Estate and Utilities even though their fundamentals are stable.

The largest single position is Software at 16% — AI infrastructure capex is still expanding and ISM new orders are confirming the cycle. The biggest weekly increase is Oil & Gas Exploration & Production, raised to 6% — capital discipline is holding for the third consecutive year, crude steady above $80/bbl, cash flow without the overinvestment trap. Trimmed: Consumer Staples to 4% (defensive premium has compressed) and Real Estate to 1% (rates path no longer trending lower).

What would change this view

The four scenarios that would force a rebalance away from this allocation.

Crude breaks below $70/bbl

Oil & Gas E&P goes from cash machine to capital trap. Capital discipline narrative collapses.

Move: Oil & Gas back to 2%. Add to Consumer Staples and Insurance for defensive carry.

Credit spreads widen > 100 bp in two weeks

Risk & Liquidity composite flips from calm to elevated. Aggressive stance no longer justified.

Move: Trim Software and Semiconductors. Raise defensive ballast from 14% to 30%.

Unemployment claims spike above 280k for three weeks

Growth composite rolls over. Retail and Aerospace & Defense repriced first; AI capex demand follows with a lag.

Move: Defensive stance. Cut equity exposure to 60%. Overweights collapse to MedDevices + Staples.

10-year yield breaks above 5.0%

Inflation & Rates composite turns stressed. Long-duration cyclicals (Real Estate, Utilities, Software) repriced lower.

Move: Software to 8%. Hold Oil & Gas and Banks (rate beneficiaries). Defensive ballast to 25%.
