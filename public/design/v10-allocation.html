Section 2 · Drivers & Themes

What's driving rotation

The Risk & Liquidity composite sits at −6.7 (calm), having improved 11 points over the quarter as credit spreads tightened and short-end funding eased. Growth is firming at +3.2, led by ISM Manufacturing finally crossing 50 and jobless claims flat-lining at trend.

The combination is what we look for to take risk: liquidity is supportive AND growth is confirming. The model takes that as a green light to lean into cyclicals with momentum (Capital Markets, Energy, Software) while trimming defensives that have already been bid up.

The dissonant note is Inflation & Rates at −1.1 (neutral) — not stressed, but not cooperative either. That's why we keep a 14% defensive ballast and why duration-sensitive sectors (Real Estate, Utilities) stay rated Less Favored despite decent fundamentals.

Themes of the month

AI infrastructure

Capex cycle still expanding. Semiconductors, cloud-data names, and the power feeders behind them all rate Most Favored or Favored.

Energy discipline

U.S. producers holding capex flat and returning cash. Crude steady above $80 means cash flow without the overinvestment trap.

Re-shoring industrials

Automation, machinery, and electrical equipment continue to benefit from supply-chain repatriation and IRA spending.

Section 5 · Risk Management

What could break this view

Crude breaks below $70/bbl

Energy goes from cash machine to capital trap. Cash flow turns vulnerable; capital discipline narrative collapses.

Alternate: rotate Energy back to Neutral, add weight to Health Care and Staples for defensive carry.

Credit spreads widen > 100 bps in two weeks

R&L composite would flip from calm to elevated. Aggressive stance no longer justified; cyclicals at risk of repricing.

Alternate: trim Capital Markets and Software, raise defensive ballast from 14% to 30%, shift stance to Balanced.

Unemployment claims spike above 280k for three weeks

Growth composite rolls over fast. Consumer Discretionary and Capital Markets repriced first; AI capex demand follows with a lag.

Alternate: shift to Defensive stance. Most Favored sectors collapse to Health Care + Staples; equity exposure cut to 60%.

10-year yield breaks above 5.0%

Inflation & Rates composite turns stressed. Long-duration cyclicals (Real Estate, Utilities, Software) repriced lower.

Alternate: cut Software to Neutral, hold Energy and Banks (rate beneficiaries), defensive ballast to 25%.
