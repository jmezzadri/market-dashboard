Macro Overview

Three volatility triggers, one cycle position, one regime read.

We describe the tape — we don't predict tops. Combined into one of four states: Risk On, Neutral, Cautionary, or Risk Off.

  • Volatility Triggers tell us when trouble has arrived.
  • Cycle Positioning tells us whether that trouble matters yet.
  • Regime is the rolled-up read across both layers.

What "Risk On" means
Vol is calm across all three triggers. The fully-invested default. No action.
To leave: any one of Equity Vol, Bond Vol, or Funding Stress crosses above its 85th-percentile level for at least one week.
NeutralOne trigger for one week.▾
What "Neutral" means
Exactly one trigger crossed for exactly one week. About one in four single-week crosses are head-fakes — they slip back below the 85th-percentile level within a week. Note it. Do not act yet.
To leave: the trigger holds for a second week → Cautionary or Risk Off. Or it slips back below → Risk On.
CautionaryTrigger sustained 2+ weeks.▾
What "Cautionary" means
A trigger has held above its 85th-percentile level for at least two weeks AND the cycle composite is at 40 or above. The cycle has either started to confirm or already broken — past the forward-looking signal point. Act on personal risk tolerance.
Risk OffSustained trigger + late-cycle.▾
What "Risk Off" means
A trigger has held for at least two weeks AND the cycle composite is below 40 (deep-calm late-expansion territory). Vol cracked while the cycle still looks fine — leading edge, not trailing. The moment broad confirmation is most likely to follow.
